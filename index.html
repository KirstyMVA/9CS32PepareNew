What is an Algorithm?

An Algorithm is simply a step-by-step set of instructions used to solve a problem or complete a task. Think of it like a recipe for baking a cake or the precise steps you take to tie your shoelaces.

Every algorithm relies on three main building blocks:

  • Input: Data given to the algorithm (e.g., ingredients).
  • Process: The actions taken (e.g., mixing and baking).
  • Output: The final result produced (e.g., a delicious cake!).

Mapping it Out with Flowcharts

Computer scientists use specific shapes to draw algorithms visually before typing them out:

  • 🔹 Oval (Terminator): Shows the START and END points of an algorithm.
  • 🔹 Rectangle (Process): Represents an action or calculation (e.g., Multiply by 2).
  • 🔹 Parallelogram (Input/Output): Represents data moving into or out of the system.
